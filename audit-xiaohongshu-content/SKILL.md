# Audit Xiaohongshu Content

Diagnose before rewriting. Preserve the author's facts and values while improving positioning, usefulness, proof, and platform fit.

## Preconditions

Require an existing draft, even if incomplete. If none is supplied, ask for it and stop; do not create from zero.

Read `references/platform-rules-and-method.md`. Use creator positioning, voice guides, and account evidence supplied with the request as account-specific context. Recheck official platform rules online when current compliance matters and browsing is available.

Treat creator/teacher claims about algorithms, viral standards, income, or fixed thresholds as attributed source claims, not platform facts. If the draft follows another creator's hit, preserve only the audience question or abstract method and rebuild from the current author's own evidence, judgment, wording, and information order.

## Establish the audit frame

Extract or infer, with clear labels:

- Primary content task: `被发现 / 建立理解 / 证明能力 / 建立信任 / 促进咨询`.
- Primary value mode: `方法价值 / 审美价值 / 情绪理解`, concrete reader gain, and optional secondary mode.
- Target user and concrete scene.
- Core conflict and single claim.
- Claim strength: classify it as `可核验事实 / 有边界的个人判断 / 变化主张 / 普遍因果`; a claim supplied by the author or brief is not evidence of itself.
- Cognitive increment: what the reader can notice, decide, or do differently and which proof earns it.
- Intended format: image-text, carousel, or video note.
- Available proof and authorization status.
- Public proof status: distinguish private source verification from material an audience can actually inspect. Retyped cards, reconstructions, stock visuals, and AI-generated graphics are explanation, not independent proof.
- Desired user action and validation metric.

Do not punish missing context twice. Mark it once as a blocker and show what evidence would resolve it.

## Audit claim strength

Apply the same ladder before scoring either platform fit or expression:

1. `可核验事实` needs a traceable dated record, artifact, authorized quote, or direct observation.
2. `有边界的个人判断` must name the event or sample and use bounded language such as `这一天让我更确定` or `目前这个样本提示`.
3. `变化主张` — including `从 X 变成 Y`, `越来越`, `不再`, and `核心工作变成了` — needs before/after or repeated evidence across time. One day's cross-section does not prove a change.
4. `普遍因果` — including `用了 X 就会 Y`, `X 导致 Y`, and universal group claims — needs broader evidence, a mechanism, and consideration of alternatives.

If proof supports a lower level only, mark the mismatch as `证据`; narrow the rewrite and show the change in the before/after table. Do not let a platform-friendly phrase bypass this check.

## Classify the problem first

Choose one primary class and up to two secondary classes:

1. `定位`: unclear target, task, promise, or account fit.
2. `选题`: weak demand, vague scene, familiar claim without a new angle, mismatch with user stage, or no useful cognitive increment.
3. `结构`: title/body mismatch, slow first three lines, multiple claims, poor sequence, or weak ending.
4. `证据`: unsupported fact, missing artifact, unclear causality, no limits, or unauthorized case.
5. `表达`: abstract, repetitive, slogan-heavy, anxiety-driven, unnatural, unlike the author, or marked by neat parallelism, fake completeness, excessive transitions, universal conclusions, and slogan-per-paragraph rhythm.
6. `平台适配`: weak search phrasing, no save-worthy object, keyword stuffing, engagement bait, hidden AI/commercial disclosure, or unsuitable metrics.

State why the primary class is upstream of the others.

## Apply hard gates

Mark the draft `不可发布` regardless of score when it contains:

- Invented or unverifiable experience, result, testimonial, platform data, or causal claim.
- A change claim without before/after or repeated evidence, or a general causal claim extrapolated from one event.
- Identifiable child, client, learner, family, or third-party material without appropriate consent.
- A false persona, undisclosed material commercial interest, or missing AI/synthetic-content disclosure when required.
- Fear, shame, conflict, extreme language, or guaranteed income used to force action.
- A title or cover claim the body cannot substantiate.
- Copied or closely paraphrased title, distinctive wording, personal story, data, screenshots, information order, or proof from another creator.
- A creator/teacher's self-reported algorithm, viral threshold, income result, or performance standard presented as a platform fact.

Do not silently repair a factual gap by inventing a more plausible detail. Use `[待确认：…]`, remove the claim, or narrow it to what is known.

Distinguish a hard gate from a readiness cap:

- No audience-visible artifact is not automatically `不可发布` when private first-party sources verify the bounded claim and the note relies on authorized narration or clearly labeled explanation. Cap the score at `84` and state the evidence limit.
- Keep `不可发布` only when the event/result is not privately verifiable, the title or body falsely promises visible proof, unauthorized material is required, or a reconstruction is presented as an original record.

## Score: 100 points

### Positioning — 15

- Primary task is explicit: 5
- Target user and scene are specific: 5
- Single claim fits the account promise: 5

### Topic — 15

- Recognizable question or demand: 6
- Timely/relevant without chasing unsupported trends: 4
- Distinct angle, value mode, and cognitive increment earned by the author's evidence: 5

### Structure — 20

- Title/cover match the content: 5
- First three lines establish scene, conflict, and payoff: 5
- Body has one dominant useful structure: 7
- Ending asks one concrete, low-pressure question: 3

### Evidence — 20

- Strong claims are traceable: 8
- Real artifacts, examples, or before/after are used: 6
- Limits and authorization are explicit: 6

### Expression — 10

- Clear, natural, concise, and voice-consistent: 5
- No empty slogans, anxiety manufacture, value distortion, or AI fingerprints: 5

### Platform fit — 20

- Natural search intent and keyword coverage: 6
- Reusable step, checklist, comparison, or template: 6
- Interaction and validation metrics fit the primary task: 4
- Current disclosure, originality, privacy, and quality rules are met: 4

Use `85-100 可进入事实复核`, `70-84 需要修改`, `<70 需要重构`. A hard-gate failure overrides the total.

After the subtotal, cap the final score at `84` when the primary claim still needs narrowing, publishable first-party proof is absent, or key authorization/disclosure remains unresolved. A private log can verify a claim internally but cannot lift the cap when the public note offers only narration, retyped cards, reconstruction, stock visuals, or AI-generated explanation. State raw subtotal, cap, and final score separately. Do not award `85+` to a draft that still needs evidence repair.

## Identify the top three problems

Rank exactly three issues by expected effect, not by ease of editing. For each give:

- Location or quoted fragment.
- Problem class and severity: `P0 阻断 / P1 高影响 / P2 中影响`.
- Why it harms discovery, understanding, proof, trust, or consultation.
- Revision principle, not merely replacement wording.

## Rewrite

1. Preserve the author's confirmed facts, viewpoint, and reasonable ambiguity.
2. Choose one primary task and one claim.
3. Rewrite title, cover, first three lines, body, proof placement, ending, keywords, and measurement plan as needed.
4. Keep useful mechanisms available to other creators.
5. Add explicit evidence slots rather than fabricated specifics.
6. Add a publication note for the platform's current AI disclosure/labeling mechanism when applicable.
7. Preserve the useful audience problem but replace borrowed expression with the author's own event, mechanism, proof, language, and sequence.

## Limit diagnosis to three rounds

Run at most three internal rounds:

1. Classify the upstream problem and apply hard gates.
2. Fix the top three issues, then rescore only affected dimensions.
3. Recheck facts, author voice, originality, AI fingerprints, privacy, value delivery, and current platform fit.

If a P0 or upstream positioning/topic/evidence gap remains, stop and return the missing source, topic decision, permission, or proof. Do not keep polishing the prose to conceal it.

## Choose output mode

- Default to `快速审核`: use exactly eight sections — `结论与六维评分 / 三个问题 / 修改原因 / 完整改稿 / 前后对照 / 证据授权待办 / 验证计划 / 硬门评分与停止判断`. Keep the whole response near or below 180 lines. Quote only the minimum fragment needed and do not restate the entire original draft.
- Use `完整审计` when the user asks for the full contract, a P0 exists, or privacy/authorization/causality requires an auditable trail. Then use every section below.
- Both modes must include a complete revised draft and may never hide unresolved evidence.

## Full output contract

Return these sections in order:

1. `审核结论` — publication status, primary problem class, primary task.
2. `逐项评分` — six dimensions, evidence for deductions, total.
3. `最影响效果的三个问题`
4. `为什么这样改`
5. `完整修改稿` — include title, cover, first three lines, full body, ending, keywords.
6. `修改前后对照` — at least five meaningful rows: title, opening, structure, proof, ending/platform fit.
7. `证据与授权待办`
8. `验证指标与下一次只改一个变量`
9. `发布前硬门复核`
10. `AI痕迹、认知增量、评论回流与迭代停止判断`

For the comment-feedback item, define what exact questions, scenes, failed attempts, and user wording to capture after publication and route upstream to audience research or topic planning. One comment is evidence, not proof of universal demand.

Do not claim the rewrite will become a hit. State what still requires real publication data.
