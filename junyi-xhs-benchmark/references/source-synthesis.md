## Incorporated principles

1. Select for reproducibility, not admiration or production polish.
2. Include recent small-account breakouts to capture currently resonant topics and structures.
3. Include accounts one stage ahead to study stable structure, cadence, trust, offers, and conversion paths.
4. Use “can the creator answer the audience's comment questions?” as a practical content-ownership check.
5. Treat the post as creator framing and the comment section as audience-language evidence.
6. Audit the trunk, branches, and leaves: primary recall, recurring themes, and concrete topics.
7. Distinguish useful, emotional, aesthetic, person/story, and conversion value.
8. Inspect whether content and back-end monetization serve the same audience problem.
9. Prefer multiple repeatable content structures over one viral outlier.
10. Translate benchmark observations into tests rather than copying finished creative work.
11. Build a scale-stratified pool: large accounts for category-scale themes and commercial systems, medium accounts for repeatable topics and series, and small accounts for current low-resource breakout signals.
12. Treat user-supplied benchmark accounts as seed nodes: extract their fingerprints and expand to adjacent accounts before judging the candidate pool complete.
13. Search through six paths: platform topic pages, target-audience identity, real problems, content mechanisms, seed-account fingerprints, and profile/comment verification.
14. For each of three to five core keywords, inspect at least one topic page in both popular and latest views and scan 30–50 posts before tracing repeated authors back to profiles.
15. Use topic pages to discover emerging accounts and themes; use 20–30 recent profile posts and comments to decide whether an account is repeatable, buyer-adjacent, and commercially aligned.
16. Separate high-recall discovery from high-precision verification. Do not use buyer or commercial-fit filters to suppress candidates during discovery.
17. Search every intended content branch, not only the commercial trunk; include post-level fingerprints such as a breakout tension, title, comment question, or visual mechanism.
18. Treat 15 verified accounts as a deliverable floor rather than a stopping rule. Track at least 25 discovered authors when access permits and require branch coverage plus two no-new-author runs before declaring saturation.
19. Preserve narrow assets as specialist benchmarks and one-off breakouts as post-level specimens instead of either inflating the whole account or silently discarding it.
20. Reconcile all user-supplied accounts against the research output and explain missed discovery, justified rejection, or role reassignment.

## Modified principles

- Treat fixed content ratios such as 20/40/20/20 as a starting hypothesis, not a universal rule.
- Treat claims about exact like/save/comment weights, hashtag counts, video uplift, timing, and algorithm changes as unverified unless current official evidence or controlled account data supports them.
- Do not infer that good content data proves product demand. Separate attention, trust, inquiry, payment, retention, and outcomes.
- Do not equate full-life disclosure with a requirement for IP. Respect privacy, consent, and unwanted-audience boundaries.
- Replace “manufacture anxiety” with accurately showing the cost of inaction without exaggeration or fear fabrication.
- Replace “洗稿” with ethical pattern transfer: borrow the audience problem, topic family, information architecture, proof pattern, or series logic while creating original wording, examples, stories, and creative assets.
