# Benchmark scorecard

## Classification before scoring

Assign one `role_scope` before scoring:

- `core`: the whole account is being considered as a direct or portfolio benchmark;
- `specialist`: the account supplies a narrow transferable asset despite broader mismatch;
- `post_specimen`: one post supplies a useful topic, hook, format, or comment-language signal without account-level repeatability.

Never apply hard gates during discovery. Gates classify verified evidence; they must not remove candidates from the discovery ledger.

## Hard gates

Use `pass`, `fail`, or `unknown`.

1. **Audience/problem match**: the account reaches a materially similar audience in a recurring situation.
2. **Current evidence**: the account or relevant posts have recent, dated evidence.
3. **Repeatable content**: several posts or a recurring series support the conclusion.
4. **Operational replicability**: success does not primarily depend on celebrity, unavailable credentials, extreme production, wealth display, or privacy exposure the user rejects.
5. **Ethical fit**: learning from the account would not require fabricated fear, hidden plagiarism, or privacy violations.

Direct benchmarks must pass gates 1–5. A specialist benchmark may fail gate 1 only when its narrow role is explicit, such as topic framing, visual packaging, trust, or conversion design. A post-level specimen may fail account-level repeatability but must have current post evidence, a clearly named transferable asset, operational replicability, and ethical fit.

## Weighted score

Score each dimension from 0 to 5.

| Dimension | Weight | A score of 5 means |
|---|---:|---|
| Audience/problem fit | 20 | Same audience moment, costly problem, and desired change |
| Position/recall clarity | 10 | A stranger can accurately retell why to follow |
| Content-system overlap | 10 | Several branches map to the user's intended content system |
| Recent attention evidence | 15 | Multiple recent posts/series show qualified attention |
| Repeatability | 10 | Performance is distributed across repeatable structures |
| Operational replicability | 15 | User can produce an adapted version with current resources |
| Trust/proof system | 10 | Both human trust and capability proof are visible |
| Commercial-path fit | 10 | Visible conversion path matches the intended business model |

Calculate `sum(score / 5 × weight)`.

Interpretation:

- 80–100: strong core benchmark;
- 65–79: useful role benchmark;
- 50–64: inspiration only;
- below 50: reject.

## Caps and cautions

- Audience/problem gate `fail`: cap a `core` candidate at 59. Do not call a specialist a direct benchmark even when its uncapped specialist score is high.
- Operational replicability gate `fail`: cap at 64.
- Current evidence `unknown`: label provisional; do not call it verified.
- One-post outlier with no repeatable series: recent-attention and repeatability scores cannot exceed 2; classify it as `post_specimen`, not an account benchmark.
- Visible service/course/link without payment evidence: score commercial-path design, but label payment `unknown`.
- Follower count alone does not increase any score.

## Portfolio rule

Do not simply take the top five totals. Assemble complementary roles. A lower-scoring conversion specialist may be more useful than a fifth direct benchmark when its role and mismatch are explicit.

Keep the three output classes separate. Core accounts answer who or what system to benchmark; specialists answer which narrow asset to study; post specimens answer which single hypothesis to test. Do not merge them into one rank.

## Tier-learning score

After the universal strategic score, score the account's assigned tier from 0 to 5 on each dimension. Keep this score separate from strategic relevance.

### Large-account learning value

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Blockbuster families, not one hit | 20 |
| Cultural-scale topic ceiling | 20 |
| IP narrative and trust architecture | 20 |
| Monetization system visibility | 20 |
| Transferable structure despite resource gap | 20 |

### Medium-account learning value

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Recurring topic system | 25 |
| Serial formats and cadence | 20 |
| Stable performance beyond outliers | 20 |
| Operational comparability | 20 |
| Content-to-offer alignment | 15 |

### Small-account learning value

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Recent breakout evidence | 25 |
| Lift over normal baseline | 20 |
| More than one supporting post | 20 |
| Low-resource reproducibility | 20 |
| Emerging audience language | 15 |

Use `sum(score / 5 × weight)`. Rank within each tier, not across tiers.

## Creator-temperament fit

Use this third, separate score when the user supplies a seed account or rejects an otherwise relevant account on style or “气质” grounds. Score 0–5 on each dimension.

| Dimension | Weight | A score of 5 means |
|---|---:|---|
| Expression style | 20 | Voice, language density, humor, directness, and spoken rhythm feel usable for the user |
| Emotional energy | 20 | Emotional temperature and vulnerability level match the user's sustainable range |
| Values/decision style | 20 | The account's visible standards, trade-offs, and way of acting fit the user's worldview |
| Lifestyle texture | 20 | Raw/polished, ordinary/aspirational, local/urban, and work/family texture are adjacent |
| Narrative stance | 20 | Peer, practitioner, teacher, observer, or authority stance matches the intended relationship |

Use `sum(score / 5 × weight)`. Treat 80+ as strong embodiment fit, 65–79 as selective fit, 50–64 as a narrow style reference, and below 50 as poor embodiment fit. Low temperament fit does not automatically reject a topic or conversion specialist, but it must change what the user is told to learn from it.
