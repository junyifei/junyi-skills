# Write Xiaohongshu Content

Create a useful, searchable, save-worthy draft without inventing facts or manipulating anxiety.

## Load context

1. Read `references/platform-rules-and-method.md` before drafting. Recheck official rules online when current compliance matters and browsing is available.
2. Use creator positioning, voice guides, and account evidence supplied with the request as account-specific context, never as universal rules.
3. Prefer user-supplied sources over remembered examples. Separate facts, inferences, hypotheses, and unknowns.
4. Treat creator/teacher claims about algorithms, viral standards, income, or fixed thresholds as attributed source claims, not platform facts.

## Route the request

- Create from notes, interviews, transcripts, evidence, research, or a brief.
- If the user supplies a finished or partial draft and asks for diagnosis, scoring, or rewriting, route to `audit-xiaohongshu-content`.
- If the input is another creator's hit note, do not paraphrase it into a disguised copy. Keep only the audience question or abstract structure, then use the current author's own event, proof, judgment, wording, and information order.
- If the core first-person event or claimed result is missing, do not fabricate a publish-ready story. Produce an evidence-ready outline with `[待确认：…]` slots and a collection list.
- If the input is an oral memory, ask or infer three questions: `为什么现在要写 / 真正踩过什么坑 / 读者看完立刻能做什么`. Label inferred answers.

## Build the content brief

Identify these fields before writing:

- Primary task: `被发现 / 建立理解 / 证明能力 / 建立信任 / 促进咨询`. Choose one primary task; optionally name one secondary task.
- Primary value mode: choose `方法价值 / 审美价值 / 情绪理解`; name the concrete value and optional secondary mode.
- Target user: role, stage, and relevant prior behavior.
- Concrete scene: when and where the problem appears.
- Search demand: the exact question or phrase the user might type.
- Core conflict: two forces that cannot both be ignored.
- Single claim: one sentence the post earns the right to make.
- Claim strength: label the claim as `可核验事实 / 有边界的个人判断 / 变化主张 / 普遍因果`. Treat a claim supplied in the brief as a hypothesis until the evidence earns that level.
- Cognitive increment: what the reader believed or did before, what changes after, and which proof earns the change.
- Proof inventory: event, source, artifact, before/after, number, quote, authorization, and date.
- Desired action: save, visit profile, ask a specific question, or describe a use case.
- Single validation hypothesis: what user response would support the claim.

Ask only for missing information that would materially change the draft. Otherwise proceed with explicit assumptions and evidence slots.

## Calibrate claim strength

Use the weakest claim that the available evidence can fully support:

1. `可核验事实`: state only what a dated record, artifact, authorized quote, or direct observation shows.
2. `有边界的个人判断`: use language such as `这一天让我更确定`, `在这个任务里我的判断是`, or `目前这个样本提示`. Tie it to the named event and keep alternatives open.
3. `变化主张`: language such as `从 X 变成 Y`, `现在不再`, `越来越`, or `核心工作变成了` requires a documented before/after comparison or repeated observations across time. A single-day cross-section is insufficient.
4. `普遍因果`: language such as `用了 X 就会 Y`, `X 导致 Y`, or `一人公司都会` requires broader evidence, a plausible mechanism, and competing explanations. Otherwise rewrite it as a bounded observation or hypothesis.

Do not inherit the brief's wording when its strength exceeds the proof. Record the downgrade in the evidence ledger.

## Apply hard gates

Stop or downgrade to an outline when any gate fails:

- A first-person experience, result, client quote, platform number, or causal claim lacks a source.
- A change claim lacks before/after or repeated evidence, or a general causal claim is inferred from one event.
- A creator/teacher's algorithm rule, viral threshold, income result, or performance standard is presented as a platform fact.
- A child, family member, client, learner, or third party is identifiable without appropriate consent.
- The copy relies on fear, shame, group conflict, fabricated scarcity, guaranteed income, or absolute outcomes.
- Commercial interest or AI assistance that should be disclosed is hidden.
- The title promises a result the body does not prove.
- The topic has no distinct event, mechanism, evidence, useful recognition, or reusable action and depends on packaging alone.
- The copy borrows another creator's title, distinctive wording, personal story, data, screenshots, information sequence, or proof.

Never turn an inference into a fact. Use calibrated language such as `这次记录显示`, `在这个任务里`, `我的当前判断`, or `还需要发布数据验证`.

Distinguish a hard gate from a readiness cap:

- Missing audience-visible proof alone is **not** a hard gate when private first-party sources verify the event, the public claim is bounded, the title does not promise visible proof, and the post uses only authorized narration or clearly labeled explanation. Keep the score at `84` or below and disclose the limitation.
- It **is** a hard gate when the underlying event/result cannot be verified even privately, the title/body claims proof the audience is not given, publication requires unauthorized material, or a reconstruction is presented as an original record.

## Draft for Xiaohongshu

### Search and user question

Anchor the post in one query cluster. Use natural variants in the title, first three lines, subheads, body, and keyword list without stuffing.

### Title and cover

Provide three title options with distinct intents: search/direct answer, conflict/curiosity, and evidence/result. Keep the promise specific and provable. Provide one short cover line and an optional subtitle.

### First three lines

Make the first three lines do three jobs in order:

1. Name the recognizable scene or result.
2. State the cost, conflict, or failed assumption.
3. Promise the useful payoff contained in the post.

Avoid biography, throat-clearing, generic motivation, and unsupported numbers.

### Body

Choose one dominant structure:

- `问题 -> 原因 -> 步骤 -> 证据 -> 边界`
- `旧做法 -> 失败 -> 新做法 -> 前后对比 -> 适用条件`
- `清单 -> 每项场景 -> 示例 -> 使用提醒`
- `模板 -> 填写方法 -> 示例 -> 常见错误`

Prefer steps, checklists, comparisons, or reusable templates. Give enough detail for another creator to apply the core method. Do not hide the useful mechanism solely to force a consultation.

Deliver the selected value mode. `方法价值` needs an applicable action or decision rule; `审美价值` needs a distinct way of seeing or presenting; `情绪理解` needs precise recognition, language, or relief rather than generic comfort. Every mode still needs an evidence-earned cognitive increment.

### Proof

Pair every strong claim with the strongest available proof: original artifact, dated record, edit trace, before/after, measured time, authorized quote, or clearly attributed source. State evidence limits.

Distinguish `source proof` from `publishable proof`. A private log, internal screen, or confidential file can verify the draft for the creator but is not automatically evidence the audience can inspect. A retyped card, anonymized reconstruction, stock visual, or AI-generated graphic explains a claim; it does not independently prove it. Count proof as publishable only when it can safely appear in the content after authorization and privacy review. State when the public-facing post relies mainly on the creator's bounded narration.

### Ending

Invite one concrete, low-pressure response that yields usable language, such as naming the task that keeps failing or the checklist item that is missing. Do not beg for likes, follows, saves, or comments.

After publication, capture exact specific questions, triggering scenes, failed attempts, and user wording from authorized comments. Send them upstream to audience research or topic planning as new evidence; do not turn one comment into a universal demand claim.

### Keywords and measurement

Provide 5-10 natural search keywords. Define one primary metric and up to three diagnostic metrics:

- Discovery: search entry and relevant impressions.
- Utility: saves and specific how-to questions.
- Relevance: profile visits and follows from the target group.
- Trust or demand: detailed comments, qualified private messages, or consultations.

Do not invent benchmarks. Compare only with the account's own same-format baseline when available.

## Choose output mode

- Default to `快速成稿`: use exactly seven compact sections — `简报与主张强度 / 标题封面与前三行 / 完整正文 / 证据隐私与公开证明 / 图文建议关键词与结尾 / 验证计划 / 硬门自评分与AI说明`. Keep the whole response near or below 140 lines. Use at most eight evidence/risk bullets and do not repeat the same facts.
- Use `完整证据稿` when the user asks for a full contract, the content has privacy/authorization/causality risk, or a team needs an auditable handoff. Then use all sections below.
- In either mode, include every unresolved hard gate. Concision never permits hiding evidence limits.

## Full output contract

Return these sections in order:

1. `内容简报`
2. `证据账本` with `事实 / 推断 / 待确认 / 不可公开`
3. `标题方案与封面文案`
4. `前三行`
5. `完整正文`
6. `图文页序或视频画面建议`
7. `搜索关键词`
8. `互动结尾与评论问题`
9. `验证指标与单一假设`
10. `发布前硬门检查`
11. `自评分`
12. `AI痕迹、原创性与评论回流检查` — remove abstract openings, neat parallelism, fake completeness, excessive transitions, universal conclusions, slogan-per-paragraph rhythm, and borrowed wording; state what comment evidence to capture after publication

Mark every unresolved item inline as `[待确认：具体问题]`. If AI assistance or synthetic media is used, include a publication note to apply the platform's current AI disclosure or labeling mechanism after human review.

## Self-score: 100 points

- Primary task, value mode, target user, and scene: 10
- Search demand and user question: 15
- Title and cover promise: 12
- First three lines: 10
- Body information structure: 14
- Reusable value and cognitive increment: 13
- Truthful proof and evidence boundaries: 15
- Ending interaction and comment-feedback question: 4
- Keywords: 3
- Validation plan: 4

Any hard-gate failure makes the draft `不可发布` regardless of score. `85+` is ready for factual review, `70-84` needs revision, and `<70` needs reframing.

Apply score ceilings after calculating the subtotal:

- Cap at `84` when the primary claim has no publishable first-party proof, still needs narrowing, or depends on unresolved authorization/disclosure. Internal source files do not remove this cap when the viewer will see only narration, retyped cards, reenactment, stock visuals, or AI-generated explanation.
- Cap at `84` when the body still shows obvious neat parallelism, fake completeness, repeated abstract conclusions, or slogan-per-paragraph rhythm.
- Mark `不可发布` rather than using a high score when any hard gate remains.

State both the raw subtotal and the final capped score, with the reason for any cap.
