# Write WeChat Channels Content

Create a human, speakable, evidence-led video—not a slogan montage, copied hit, or fabricated drama.

## Load context

1. Read `references/platform-rules-and-method.md` before drafting. Recheck the official dynamic rules online when browsing is available.
2. Use creator positioning, voice guides, and account evidence supplied with the request as account-specific context. Keep them separate from general platform rules.
3. Prefer first-party records over public samples. Label facts, inferences, hypotheses, and unknowns.
4. Treat creator/teacher claims about algorithms, viral standards, revenue, or fixed thresholds as `source claims`, not platform facts. Use them only as hypotheses unless current official or account-level evidence verifies them.

## Route the request

- Create from a real event, interview, transcript, source bundle, or content brief.
- If the user supplies an existing draft and asks for diagnosis, scoring, or rewriting, route to `audit-wechat-channels-content`.
- If the input is another creator's hit video, do not paraphrase it into a disguised copy. Extract only the audience question or structural hypothesis, then require the current creator's own event, judgment, evidence, wording, and shot sequence.
- If no real event or proof exists for a first-person claim, produce an evidence-ready script with `[待确认：…]` slots. Do not invent a scene, dialogue, result, or family reaction.
- If the source is an oral memory rather than a brief, ask or infer three questions: `为什么现在要讲 / 当时真正踩了什么坑 / 观众看完立刻能做什么`. Label any inferred answer.

## Build the content brief

Define:

- Primary task: `被发现 / 建立理解 / 证明能力 / 建立信任 / 促进咨询`.
- Primary value mode: choose one of `方法价值 / 审美价值 / 情绪理解`; name an optional secondary mode and the concrete value the viewer receives.
- Target viewer and the relationship in which the video matters.
- Concrete event with date/context.
- Event chronology: confirmed actions in order, source for each action, and unknown intervals. Do not fill gaps with plausible transitions.
- First-three-second conflict: person plus relationship/responsibility tension.
- One-sentence inner voice the target viewer might privately think.
- Core conflict and single claim.
- Claim strength: label it `可核验事实 / 有边界的个人判断 / 变化主张 / 普遍因果`. A worldview or claim supplied in the brief remains a hypothesis until the event evidence earns it.
- Choice: what the person actually did or refused to do.
- Consequence: what changed, cost, or remained unresolved.
- Human judgment: what could not be outsourced to a tool or system.
- Cognitive increment: what the viewer believed/noticed before and what this event lets them see or do differently.
- Proof inventory and authorization.
- Intended recipient: who a viewer may want to send it to, and what they should discuss.
- Single validation hypothesis.

Ask only for missing information that changes the story materially. Otherwise proceed with labeled assumptions.

## Calibrate claim strength

Use the weakest level the evidence fully earns:

1. `可核验事实`: a dated record, artifact, authorized quote, or direct observation supports the statement.
2. `有边界的个人判断`: bind language such as `这一天让我更确定` or `在这个任务里我看到` to one event and leave alternatives open.
3. `变化主张`: `从 X 变成 Y`, `越来越`, `不再`, or `核心工作变成了` requires a documented before/after or repeated observations across time. A single-day event cannot establish the change by itself.
4. `普遍因果`: `用了 X 就会 Y`, `X 导致 Y`, or claims about all founders/parents require broader evidence, mechanism, and competing explanations. Otherwise downgrade to a bounded judgment or hypothesis.

Do not preserve a strong slogan merely because it was supplied as the `唯一主张`. Record any downgrade in the event ledger and make the spoken conclusion match it.

## Apply hard gates

Stop or downgrade to an outline when:

- The event, dialogue, result, testimonial, platform number, or causal claim lacks evidence.
- A change claim lacks before/after or repeated evidence, or a general causal claim is inferred from one event.
- An action, reaction, sequence, or transition such as `那天晚上 / 第一反应 / 回家后 / 先…再…` is unsupported by the event record.
- A child, family member, client, learner, or third party is identifiable without appropriate consent.
- The story uses staged conflict presented as real, false misery, group antagonism, fear, shame, or guaranteed outcomes.
- AI-generated/synthetic or fictionalized material is not planned for prominent disclosure under current rules.
- The opening or caption exaggerates beyond the actual video.
- The content is a low-quality text slideshow, repeated batch variant, unrelated voiceover, or copied material.
- The topic has no distinct event, mechanism, evidence, or useful cognitive increment and only becomes attractive through packaging.
- A creator/teacher's self-reported algorithm rule, income result, viral threshold, or performance standard is presented as a platform fact.

Distinguish a hard gate from a readiness cap:

- Missing audience-visible proof alone is **not** a hard gate when private first-party sources verify the event, the spoken claim is bounded, the title does not promise original footage, and the final cut uses authorized narration plus honestly labeled explanation. Cap the score at `84` and state that trust still needs publication data.
- It **is** a hard gate when the event/result cannot be verified privately, the video claims or implies original proof it does not have, publication requires unauthorized material, or reenactment/generic/AI footage is presented as the event record.

## Write the Channels story

### First three seconds

Start with a person in a responsibility or relationship conflict. The first action must be a confirmed event action, a confirmed artifact, or an explicitly labeled `事后演示`. If none exists, open with the evidence artifact or a bounded statement; never manufacture drama. Avoid self-introduction, abstract era commentary, and unearned extreme claims.

### One-sentence inner voice

Write one short line that the target viewer could say privately. It should reveal tension, not manufacture panic.

### Real event arc

Use this sequence unless the source calls for a different one:

1. `事件`: what actually happened.
2. `反差`: what was expected versus what occurred.
3. `选择`: what the person did, stopped, or protected.
4. `后果`: what became lighter, harder, clearer, or still unresolved.
5. `人的判断`: the responsibility, relationship, or value that remained human.

Do not force a warm ending if the evidence is mixed. Honest uncertainty can build trust.

### Protect event and shot truth

Build an event ledger before writing. Assign every proposed visual one truth tag:

- `真实记录`: the source directly supports that the action happened then; cite the record.
- `事后演示`: filmed later only to explain a method; label it on screen and never imply it happened in the event.
- `通用示意`: a non-event visual that illustrates a general concept; do not attach it to the person's chronology.
- `待确认`: not publish-ready and cannot appear as a factual action.

Never turn B-roll into event chronology. A sensible reconstruction remains a reconstruction. Keep unknown time gaps unknown, and remove any unsupported first reaction, order, location, or consequence.

### Turn macro judgment into an event

When the source starts with a worldview such as `AI不能替人决定`, bind it to one confirmed choice, competing option, consequence, and visible proof. If no event earns the judgment, return an evidence-ready outline instead of polishing the slogan.

### Spoken rhythm

- Write for breath and speech, not essay reading.
- Keep most sentences short; vary length for emphasis.
- Put one idea in each beat.
- Use concrete nouns and verbs; remove stacked abstractions.
- Read aloud and remove tongue-twisting phrases, repeated transitions, and slogan piles.
- Suggest a duration range based on story needs, not a claimed universal optimum.

### Shots and proof

For each beat specify `person/action`, `environment`, `proof artifact`, and `privacy treatment`. Use original work screens, edit marks, timelines, task boards, before/after, measured records, or authorized material. Do not use children or clients as emotional props.

Add the truth tag and source ID to every beat. Do not write a suggested prop, screen operation, or reaction as something that already happened.

Distinguish `source proof` from `publishable proof`. A private log or internal screen may verify the script for the creator but cannot count as audience-visible proof unless it is authorized, safely redacted, and actually planned for the final cut. A reenacted screen, retyped status card, generic B-roll, or AI-generated visual is explanatory footage, not independent evidence of the event.

### Ending and relationship direction

Name the relevant relationship and one discussion question. A non-coercive ending may say who this is worth watching with or sending to. Never reward, threaten, shame, or manipulate the viewer into sharing, following, liking, or commenting.

### Measurement

Choose one primary metric and up to four diagnostics:

- Relationship spread: shares/forwards and relationship-specific comments.
- Story delivery: completion and meaningful watch behavior available in the creator backend.
- Person/positioning: follows and repeat viewers.
- Trust/demand: specific questions, qualified consultations, or private messages.

Do not invent a benchmark or claim that any metric controls recommendation. Use the account's own same-format baseline when available.

## Choose output mode

- Default to `快速成稿`: use exactly eight compact sections — `简报与主张强度 / 标题封面前三秒与心声 / 完整口播 / 镜头证明表 / 结尾关系方向 / 证据隐私与公开证明 / 验证计划 / 硬门自评分与AI说明`. Keep the whole response near or below 160 lines and the shot table to at most eight rows. Do not repeat the event in the brief, ledger, script, and checklist.
- Use `完整证据稿` when the user asks for the full contract, the story has privacy/authorization/chronology/causality risk, or a production team needs an auditable handoff. Then use every section below.
- Every mode must preserve truth tags and unresolved hard gates.

## Full output contract

Return:

1. `内容简报`
2. `证据与事件时间线账本` with `事实 / 推断 / 待确认 / 不可公开`, chronology, and source IDs
3. `标题与封面文案` — three title options and one cover line
4. `前三秒` — words, action, and first frame
5. `一句话心声`
6. `完整口播稿` — with beat or time markers
7. `镜头与证明材料表` — every row includes `真实记录 / 事后演示 / 通用示意 / 待确认` and a source
8. `结尾：转给谁、讨论什么`
9. `验证指标与单一假设`
10. `发布前硬门检查`
11. `自评分`
12. `AI痕迹与原创性自检` — remove abstract openings, neat slogan piles, fake completeness, excessive transitions, universal conclusions, borrowed wording, and unsupported concrete actions

Add `[待确认：…]` inline where needed. If AI assistance, synthetic media, or fictionalized performance is involved, include a publication note to use the current prominent labeling mechanism after human review.

## Self-score: 100 points

- Primary task, value mode, target viewer, and relationship: 10
- First-three-second conflict and opening truth: 12
- One-sentence inner voice: 7
- Real event, chronology, evidence, and source boundaries: 20
- Contrast, choice, consequence, human judgment, and cognitive increment: 15
- Spoken rhythm, voice, and low AI fingerprint: 10
- Shots, proof materials, and truth tags: 11
- Relationship-directed ending: 5
- Validation metrics: 5
- Current platform compliance: 5

Any hard-gate failure makes the draft `不可发布`. `85+` is ready for factual and read-aloud review, `70-84` needs revision, and `<70` needs reframing.

Apply score ceilings after calculating the subtotal:

- Cap at `84` when the primary claim lacks publishable first-party proof, still needs narrowing, or depends on unresolved authorization/disclosure. Internal records do not lift the cap when the planned cut shows only narration, reenactment, retyped cards, generic B-roll, or AI-generated explanation.
- Cap at `84` when the script still reads as a roster/status report, neat slogan sequence, essay, or falsely complete story rather than an event with a human choice.
- Mark `不可发布` rather than using a high score when any hard gate remains.

State raw subtotal, final capped score, and the cap reason.
