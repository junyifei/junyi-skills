# WeChat Channels platform rules and method

Last checked: 2026-07-17. The official operating standard is dynamic.

## Evidence labels

- `Official rule`: hard compliance boundary.
- `Official product material`: evidence about the named feature or advertising context, not an organic algorithm formula.
- `External public sample`: supports a storytelling hypothesis only.
- `Account result`: required to validate performance.
- `Creator/teacher source claim`: a named person's experience or interpretation. It may suggest a test, but it is not a platform rule, algorithm fact, universal viral standard, or verified income result.

## Current official rules

The current `微信视频号运营规范` requires honest use and safe content. Relevant clauses prohibit or restrict:

- Fake registration, misleading identity, fake data, deceptive content, false publicity, and undisclosed advertising.
- Rewarded, coerced, threatening, or inflammatory requests to share, follow, like, or comment.
- Exaggerated titles, title/content mismatch, fabricated or sensational stories, fake misery, emotional incitement, and rapid-income mythology.
- Copyright, portrait, privacy, personal-information, and business-secret violations.
- Plagiarism, moving/reposting, or other infringement. Borrow a demand question or abstract method only; do not copy another creator's wording, story, screenshots, shot order, distinctive sequence, or result claims.
- Disclosure of minors' privacy or content harmful to minors. The rules specifically call for caution and rights-holder consent when publishing a minor's name, likeness, voice, or behavior.
- Batch publication of near-identical fabricated content and low-quality videos such as text slideshows, unclear video, unrelated audio/visuals, or misleading edits.
- AI-generated/synthetic non-real audio/video or fictionalized performance without a prominent label. The platform may add visible or invisible labels itself.

Official source: https://weixin.qq.com/cgi-bin/readtemplate?lang=zh_CN&t=weixin_agreement&s=video

## Public sample boundary

- The reference snapshot dated 2026-07-17 used Tencent advertising materials, public case pages, Newrank, and YouWant. It did not include an auditable logged-in Channels keyword-results page.
- YouWant's 2026 public rankings explicitly say its follower estimates and index are based on its collected sample and model, not real-time creator data. Use these panels to identify possible cases, never as authoritative account data.
- Older Tencent advertising materials and public cases support the hypothesis that sharing in WeChat can express relationship and identity. They do not prove an organic recommendation formula in 2026.
- Recent 2026 public sample coverage remains stronger for rankings, commerce, and large creators than for the exact overlap of AI + founder-mother + family. Treat the gap as uncertainty, not proof of an empty niche.

## Method hypotheses to validate

- A person/relationship/responsibility conflict can make the first seconds legible.
- A single inner voice can invite identification without abstract preaching.
- Event, contrast, choice, consequence, and human judgment create a shareable relationship story.
- `转给谁` is a relationship-direction prompt, not permission to coerce engagement.
- Shares, completion, relationship comments, follows, and qualified consultations diagnose different jobs; none should be treated as the sole platform goal.

## Operational boundary

Do not claim a universal best duration, guaranteed completion rate, fixed algorithm weight, or in-app trend without an auditable source. State that Channels in-app search/ranking data was unavailable when that remains true.

When learning from a public hit, use `same demand, different evidence`: create from the current author's own event, proof, judgment, language, and shot design. A close paraphrase is not made original by changing synonyms.
