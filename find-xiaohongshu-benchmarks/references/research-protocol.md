# Research protocol

## 1. Benchmark brief

Capture this minimum brief before searching:

| Field | Question |
|---|---|
| Recall trigger | When what happens, to whom, should they remember the creator? |
| Reach audience | Who may watch and follow? |
| Paying buyer | Who has the costly problem and can buy? |
| Transformation | What observable before/after is promised? |
| Commercial anchor | What product or service is adjacent? |
| Content branches | Which recurring themes all return to the same trunk? |
| Production constraints | What formats, frequency, team, privacy, and scenes are realistic? |
| Current stage | New account, early traction, stable small account, or mature account? |

## 2. Search matrix and six-path loop

Build at least three terms in each relevant pool:

- identity: `创业妈妈`, `一人公司`, `女性创业者`;
- scene/problem: `什么都要自己扛`, `创始人太累`, `当妈后事业`;
- desired change: `不再内耗`, `事业家庭不二选一`, `让AI接住工作`;
- mechanism: `AI Agent`, `修心`, `关系修复`, `支持系统`;
- content format: `创业vlog`, `真实记录`, `案例复盘`;
- offer: `陪跑`, `咨询`, `社群`, `课程`.

Search category terms and natural-language combinations. Record each query and why it exists. Use related-search suggestions and comments to expand wording, but keep the original search log.

Separate discovery from evaluation:

1. In discovery, optimize for recall. Record any author or post that matches at least one trunk, branch, format, temperament, trust, or conversion role.
2. In verification, optimize for precision. Apply audience, buyer, repeatability, operational, and commercial-fit checks.

Do not reject an account during discovery merely because its bio, main audience, or offer is broader than the user's positioning. Record the triggering post or series and decide later whether the asset belongs at account level or post level.

Build coverage for both:

- **trunk lanes**: paying buyer, costly problem, promised change, commercial mechanism;
- **branch lanes**: every intended content branch plus its identity, life-stage, emotion, worldview, story, and format variants.

For every content branch, run at least two distinct combinations when access permits: one identity/problem query and one mechanism/format or desired-change query. Example: a branch about AI and life choices should search `AI人生规划`, `AI职业迷茫`, or `AI替我做决定`, not only `AI员工` or `一人公司`.

Choose three to five **core keywords** from the brief: the highest-priority terms that connect the paying buyer, recurring problem, and creator mechanism. Run all relevant discovery paths below instead of relying on one category noun such as `AI博主`.

### Path 1: Platform topic-page scan

For every core keyword:

1. enter at least one relevant Xiaohongshu topic page;
2. inspect both popular and latest views;
3. scan 30–50 posts, or every visible post when fewer than 30 are available;
4. log repeated authors and recurring topic, hook, format, and comment-language patterns;
5. prioritize opening small accounts with unusually strong interaction and medium accounts with stable performance across several posts;
6. trace promising posts back to the creator's profile.

Use topic pages for discovery, not final selection. A topic-page appearance or one strong post never substitutes for profile verification. Record the keyword, topic-page URL, views checked, post count, repeated authors, and access limits.

### Path 2: Target-audience search

Combine audience identity with its work or life context to find buyer-adjacent accounts. Examples include `创业妈妈怎么用AI`, `女老板AI`, `一人公司妈妈`, `女性创业者AI`, `创始人太累`, and `所有决定都要老板做`. Prefer phrases the intended buyer would use about herself over creator category labels.

### Path 3: Real-problem search

Search complete questions and frustrations, such as `AI为什么让我更累`, `AI生成很多但还是要我审核`, `怎么让AI懂我的公司`, `创始人经验怎么沉淀`, `不会授权怎么办`, and `AI替我干活`. Use this path to discover both accounts and content-theme hypotheses. Do not force every useful problem query to yield a benchmark account.

### Path 4: Content-mechanism search

Search how the promised change is produced: `AI团队`, `AI员工`, `Agent翻车`, `AI工作流实测`, `一人公司工作台`, `知识库搭建`, `训练AI`, and `AI学会我`. Record whether the account teaches tools, demonstrates a repeatable operating system, or connects the mechanism to a costly buyer problem.

### Path 5: Seed-account expansion

Use the seed-account procedure below. Search each extracted fingerprint separately—such as audience identity, knowledge base, Agent/Skill, multi-account operations, low-priced guide/service, or speaking-and-cover format—instead of searching only the creator's name.

Expand both account fingerprints and post fingerprints. Account fingerprints describe the creator's stable system; post fingerprints include a triggering title, tension, story, comment question, visual mechanism, or one-off breakout theme. This prevents a cross-category creator with one highly relevant post from disappearing from the research.

### Path 6: Profile and comment verification

Inspect 20–30 recent posts for every serious candidate and determine:

- whether updates are current and stable;
- which topic families recur;
- whether strong performance is distributed or rests on one post;
- the account's normal interaction range and the lift of stronger posts;
- who appears in the comments and how they describe themselves;
- whether comments ask only about tools or about applying the ideas to their own company or situation;
- whether consultation, product, community, case, or other commercial signals exist;
- whether the content audience and back-end offer serve the same people and problem.

Do not promote a discovered account into the scored pool until this verification is complete or explicitly marked incomplete. When only one post is relevant, preserve it as a `post-level specimen` instead of treating the entire account as repeatable.

## 3. Discovery ledger, saturation, and sampling

Maintain a discovery ledger with one row per surfaced author or post:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Account/post | Preserve the exact author and triggering asset |
| Discovery source | Query, topic page, comment, related search, or seed fingerprint |
| Coverage lane | Trunk or named content branch |
| Provisional role | Direct, breakout, trust, conversion, specialist, or post specimen |
| Verification status | Unchecked, partial, or verified |
| Early mismatch | Record possible mismatch without deleting the candidate |

Use these stopping rules when access permits:

1. discover at least 25 plausible authors before narrowing;
2. cover every content branch with at least two distinct query combinations;
3. inspect popular and latest views for every core keyword;
4. continue until two consecutive discovery runs in distinct lanes yield no new plausibly useful authors.

If any rule is unmet, label discovery `not saturated` and list the missing lane or access limit.

- Target at least 25 discovered authors, 15 verified candidates, and 12 scored candidates when platform access permits.
- Default to five large, five medium, and five small accounts. Use `large ≥ 500k`, `medium 50k–499k`, and `small < 50k` as working bands unless the niche requires relative bands.
- Treat follower bands as sampling structure, not evidence of quality, customer value, or revenue.
- Search more than one keyword lane so personalization does not define the whole pool.
- Do not count repeated result slots as unique authors. Track both result slots scanned and unique authors discovered.
- Cover all six paths that are applicable to the brief. Log any skipped path and the reason.
- For each core keyword, complete one topic-page scan of 30–50 posts across popular and latest views. If access or supply prevents this, record the actual count and limitation.
- Date observations and preserve direct profile or post URLs.
- Prefer recent 20–30-post profile samples. State the exact sample if smaller.
- Note the median-like normal range qualitatively or quantitatively; do not let the highest post represent the account.
- Mark a recent breakout only when the post date and account scale are both observed.
- Mark commercial validation only with documented payment, repeat purchase, referral, or equivalent costly commitment. A bio link or course mention is only a commercial signal.

### Tier-specific collection

For large accounts, collect:

- at least three blockbuster families rather than isolated posts;
- recurring mass-market tensions and narrative devices;
- trust and monetization architecture;
- production or identity advantages the user cannot reproduce.

For medium accounts, collect:

- recurring topic families and series;
- normal-performance range versus stronger posts;
- cadence, format, information density, and conversion path;
- which structures the user can execute with current resources.

For small accounts, collect:

- post date and follower snapshot;
- lift over the account's normal range;
- whether more than one post supports the pattern;
- low-cost format, emerging language, and likely audience trigger.

### Seed-account expansion

When the user gives a benchmark account:

1. verify the exact account name and ID;
2. inspect its profile and a recent post sample;
3. extract eight fingerprints: audience, identity, problem, desired result, topic families, format, creator temperament, monetization;
4. express creator temperament through five observable axes: expression style, emotional energy, values/decision style, lifestyle texture, and narrative stance;
5. search each fingerprint separately;
6. inspect related accounts, co-mentioned creators, comment-language terms, and repeated topic phrases;
7. explain which fingerprint made each new candidate adjacent.
8. add every user-supplied account to a reconciliation table, even if the final decision is specialist, post specimen, or reject;
9. when a user-supplied account was absent from an earlier run, identify whether the cause was an uncovered branch, missing post fingerprint, personalized ranking, access limitation, premature saturation, or a justified exclusion.

## 4. Evidence record

Use one row per candidate:

| Field | Required evidence |
|---|---|
| Identity | account name, Xiaohongshu ID if visible, profile URL |
| Snapshot | observation date, follower count if visible, recent activity |
| Position | bio plus recurring content evidence |
| Audience/problem | post topics and audience language in comments |
| Repeatability | multiple posts/series, not one outlier |
| Replicability | format, scene, team, identity and resource requirements |
| Trust | story, lived scenes, process, cases, artifacts, outcomes |
| Commercial path | offer, consultation, shop, live, community, case or CTA signals |
| Creator temperament | voice, emotional energy, values, lifestyle texture, teacher/peer/observer stance |
| Limits | unknowns, inaccessible evidence, confounders |

Label claims:

- `fact`: directly visible or documented;
- `inference`: reasoned from multiple facts;
- `hypothesis`: plausible but untested;
- `unknown`: required evidence is missing.

## 5. Comment mining

Record repeated or high-signal comments under four buckets:

1. self-description: “this is me” and lived scenes;
2. unresolved question: what they still need explained;
3. objection: where the framing loses trust;
4. buying signal: asking about service, price, course, consultation, or next step.

High-liked comments reveal resonant language, not market size or payment. Paraphrase unless a short exact phrase is essential.

## 6. Browser fallback

If Xiaohongshu blocks search, requires sign-in, or hides profile data:

- ask the user to sign in when necessary;
- do not replace missing platform evidence with unsupported confidence;
- use external search only for discovery and label it as such;
- return a provisional list with explicit missing checks.
