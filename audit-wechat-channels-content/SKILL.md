# Audit WeChat Channels Content

Diagnose the existing draft before rewriting it. Improve delivery without inventing a drama or changing the author's values.

## Preconditions

Require an existing draft, even if partial. If none is supplied, ask for the draft and stop; do not create from zero.

Read `references/platform-rules-and-method.md`. Use creator positioning, voice guides, and account evidence supplied with the request as account-specific context. Recheck the official dynamic rules online when current compliance matters and browsing is available.

Treat creator/teacher claims about algorithms, viral standards, revenue, or fixed thresholds as attributed source claims, never as platform facts. If the draft closely follows another creator's hit, preserve only the audience question or abstract method and rebuild from the author's own event, proof, judgment, wording, and shots.

## Establish the audit frame

Extract or infer, with labels:

- Primary task: `被发现 / 建立理解 / 证明能力 / 建立信任 / 促进咨询`.
- Primary value mode: `方法价值 / 审美价值 / 情绪理解`, concrete viewer gain, and any secondary mode.
- Target viewer and relevant relationship.
- Real event, core conflict, and single claim.
- Claim strength: classify it as `可核验事实 / 有边界的个人判断 / 变化主张 / 普遍因果`; do not treat a supplied worldview or unique claim as evidence.
- Confirmed event chronology, unknown intervals, and source for each action/reaction.
- One-sentence inner voice.
- Choice, consequence, and human judgment.
- Cognitive increment: what the viewer can notice, decide, or do differently after the event.
- Proof shots, source records, and authorization.
- Public proof status: separate internal source verification from material the audience can inspect. Reenactments, retyped cards, generic B-roll, and AI-generated visuals explain but do not independently prove the event.
- Intended recipient and discussion question.
- Primary metric and validation hypothesis.

Mark missing context once as a blocker and name the evidence that would resolve it.

## Audit claim strength

Run this check before rewarding story structure or spoken impact:

1. `可核验事实` needs a traceable dated record, artifact, authorized quote, or direct observation.
2. `有边界的个人判断` must name the event/sample and stay bounded, for example `这一天让我更确定` or `在这次任务里我看到`.
3. `变化主张` — `从 X 变成 Y`, `越来越`, `不再`, or `核心工作变成了` — needs before/after or repeated evidence across time. One day does not prove a change.
4. `普遍因果` — `用了 X 就会 Y`, `X 导致 Y`, or universal founder/parent claims — needs broader evidence, mechanism, and competing explanations.

If the evidence earns a lower level only, classify the mismatch as `证据`, even when `结构` is also weak. Narrow the full rewrite and include the change in the before/after comparison. Apply the same threshold used by the Xiaohongshu audit; platform style never lowers the truth threshold.

## Classify the problem first

Choose one primary class and up to two secondary classes:

1. `定位`: unclear target, task, person label, promise, or account fit.
2. `选题`: no recognizable relationship/responsibility tension, vague scene, weak relevance, no distinct evidence, or no cognitive increment.
3. `结构`: slow first three seconds, no event arc, multiple messages, missing choice/consequence, or weak ending.
4. `证据`: invented/unsupported event, dialogue, action order, first reaction, result, causal claim, platform number, or unauthorized material.
5. `表达`: written like an essay, slogan-stacked, hard to speak, manipulative, unlike the author, or marked by abstract openings, neat parallelism, fake completeness, excessive transitions, or universal conclusions.
6. `平台适配`: weak first frame, no person/relationship direction, no proof shots, engagement coercion, title mismatch, low-quality repetition, or missing AI/fiction label.

Explain why the primary class is upstream.

## Apply hard gates

Mark `不可发布` regardless of score when the draft contains:

- Unverifiable experience, dialogue, result, testimonial, platform data, or causal claim.
- A change claim without before/after or repeated evidence, or a general causal claim extrapolated from one event.
- A plausible reenactment, suggested B-roll action, location, order, transition, or reaction presented as something that happened then.
- Identifiable child, client, learner, family, or third-party material without appropriate consent.
- Staged conflict presented as real, fake misery, false persona, undisclosed material commercial interest, or guaranteed outcome.
- Fear, shame, group antagonism, or coercive engagement used for reach.
- Missing prominent AI/synthetic/fiction disclosure when current rules require it.
- A title/opening not supported by the video, copied material, unrelated audio/visuals, or near-identical batch content.
- A creator/teacher's self-reported algorithm, income, viral threshold, or performance standard presented as a platform fact.

Never repair a factual gap by making the scene more vivid. Remove, narrow, or mark `[待确认：…]`.

Distinguish a hard gate from a readiness cap:

- No audience-visible original artifact is not automatically `不可发布` when private first-party sources verify the bounded event and the final cut uses authorized narration plus honestly labeled explanation. Cap the score at `84`; treat the missing public artifact as a trust/readiness limitation.
- Keep `不可发布` when the event/result is not privately verifiable, the title/video falsely promises original proof, unauthorized material is necessary, or reenactment/generic/AI footage is presented as the event record.

## Score: 100 points

### Positioning — 15

- Primary task is explicit: 5
- Target viewer and relationship are specific: 5
- Single claim fits the account promise: 5

### Topic — 15

- Recognizable real scene and tension: 6
- Viewer has a reason to care or discuss: 4
- Distinct angle, value mode, and cognitive increment are earned by the author's evidence: 5

### Structure — 20

- First three seconds show person + relationship/responsibility conflict: 6
- One-sentence inner voice is clear: 3
- Event -> contrast -> choice -> consequence -> judgment arc: 8
- Ending names recipient and discussion: 3

### Evidence — 20

- Event, chronology, dialogue, and results are traceable: 8
- Proof shots, truth tags, or artifacts are planned: 6
- Limits, privacy, and authorization are explicit: 6

### Expression — 10

- Speakable rhythm, short beats, and concrete words: 5
- Voice and values are preserved without anxiety, slogan piles, or AI fingerprints: 5

### Platform fit — 20

- Title, cover, first frame, and content agree: 5
- Person/action/environment/proof shots fit the story and are truth-tagged: 5
- Relationship-directed CTA and validation metrics fit the task: 5
- Current originality, disclosure, privacy, minors, and quality rules are met: 5

Use `85-100 可进入事实与口播复核`, `70-84 需要修改`, `<70 需要重构`. A hard-gate failure overrides the score.

After the subtotal, cap the final score at `84` when the primary claim still needs narrowing, publishable first-party proof is absent, or key authorization/disclosure remains unresolved. A private log can verify the script internally but cannot lift the cap when the final cut offers only narration, reenactment, retyped cards, generic B-roll, or AI-generated explanation. State raw subtotal, cap, and final score separately. Do not award `85+` while evidence repair remains.

## Identify the top three problems

Rank exactly three issues by expected effect. For each provide:

- Location or quoted fragment.
- Class and severity: `P0 阻断 / P1 高影响 / P2 中影响`.
- Why it damages delivery, completion, relationship sharing, trust, or consultation.
- Revision principle.

## Rewrite

1. Preserve confirmed facts, viewpoint, uncertainty, and values.
2. Commit to one task, one event, and one claim.
3. Rewrite title, cover, first three seconds, inner voice, event arc, spoken beats, proof shots, ending, and metrics.
4. Keep methods transferable to other creators.
5. Insert evidence slots instead of invented specificity.
6. Include the current AI/synthetic/fiction labeling note when applicable.
7. Read aloud conceptually: remove essay syntax, repeated connectors, and phrases that cannot be spoken naturally.
8. Tag every shot as `真实记录 / 事后演示 / 通用示意 / 待确认` and cite its source. Never turn a later demonstration into event chronology.
9. Bind any macro judgment to one confirmed event, choice, consequence, and proof. If the event is absent, return it upstream instead of polishing the worldview.

## Limit diagnosis to three rounds

Run at most three internal rounds:

1. Classify the upstream problem and apply hard gates.
2. Fix the top three issues, then rescore only affected dimensions.
3. Recheck fact/chronology/shot truth, author voice, privacy, originality, and current platform fit.

If a P0 or upstream positioning/topic/evidence gap remains, stop iterating and return the missing fact, choice, permission, or topic decision. Do not keep rewriting prose to conceal an upstream defect.

## Choose output mode

- Default to `快速审核`: use exactly nine sections — `结论与六维评分 / 三个问题 / 修改原因 / 完整改稿 / 镜头证明表 / 前后对照 / 证据授权待办 / 验证计划 / 硬门评分与停止判断`. Keep the whole response near or below 190 lines and the shot table to at most eight rows. Quote only minimal original fragments and avoid repeating the same explanation.
- Use `完整审计` when the user asks for the full contract, a P0 exists, or privacy/authorization/chronology/causality needs an auditable trail. Then use all sections below.
- Both modes must include a complete revised script and may not hide unresolved evidence.

## Full output contract

Return:

1. `审核结论` — publication status, primary problem class, primary task.
2. `逐项评分` — six dimensions, deduction evidence, total.
3. `最影响效果的三个问题`
4. `为什么这样改`
5. `完整修改稿` — title, cover, first three seconds, inner voice, full voiceover with beats, ending.
6. `镜头与证明材料表`
7. `修改前后对照` — at least five rows: title, opening, event arc, evidence, ending/platform fit.
8. `证据与授权待办`
9. `验证指标与下一次只改一个变量`
10. `发布前硬门复核`
11. `AI痕迹、认知增量与迭代停止判断`

Do not call the revision a likely hit. Explicitly state what still requires real Channels publication data.
