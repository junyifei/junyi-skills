# Generalization tests

## Contents

## 1. Purpose

These synthetic boundary tests check that the skill is not hard-coded to one niche, identity, or creator. They test the same contracts across creator categories. They are workflow tests, not claims about real market demand.

## 2. Test cases

| Creator | Candidate | Evidence condition | Expected judgment | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| B2B sales consultant, strict shareability | “I rejected a high-reply outreach template after 50 qualified leads” | CRM export, message variants, lead quality, repeated sales-team scene, named forwarding recipient | Keep | Counterintuitive quality-over-reply judgment, shared scene, forwarding job, and proof all pass |
| B2B sales consultant | “The 10 hottest AI sales tools this month” | No first-party use; copied trend list | Eliminate | Tool collectors, borrowed authority, unrelated to proven method |
| Registered dietitian | “Why my client’s 4pm hunger did not improve by adding willpower” | Credential, consented anonymized diary, bounded nutrition claim | Keep or rewrite | Qualification and case exist; wording must avoid diagnosis/guarantees |
| Lifestyle creator without medical qualification | “This symptom means your hormones are broken” | Anecdote only | Eliminate | Qualification and integrity gates fail regardless of heat |
| Product photographer, task-calibrated trust | “The shot list mistake that forced a full reshoot” | Original brief, failed set, revised checklist, client permission | Keep with narrow-role basis | Search/save utility and proof justify a narrow trust role even if counterintuitive framing needs work; do not claim broad propagation |
| Founder, strict shareability | “Eight agents audited my website” | Full first-party logs, but no repeated target-user website-audit scene | Rewrite | True proof does not establish a shared scene; re-angle to a repeated founder job such as reviewing AI-generated outputs |
| Product photographer | “Five viral angles every brand must use” | No campaign data; same advice as prior posts | Eliminate | Empty universal claim and duplicate pseudo-topic |
| Parenting creator | “The stroller feature I regretted paying for after 30 days” | Receipt, 30-day use log, specific family context | Keep | First-party case and decision intent; target audience is clear |
| Parenting creator | “AI will make your child unemployable” | News reaction only | Eliminate | Anxiety, unsupported forecast, wrong authority |

## 3. Conclusion

The workflow is portable when five inputs are replaced per creator: positioning, target/excluded audience, proof inventory, comparable baselines, and distribution policy. The three distribution gates prevent a true but niche creator artifact from being mistaken for a broadly shareable topic. The six core gates and ten dimensions remain stable across expertise, lifestyle, and product-use creators.

The skill should not be considered domain expertise. High-stakes medical, legal, financial, child-development, or safety claims still require the creator's legitimate qualification and relevant review. Without that, the correct outcome is `eliminate` or a narrower first-person experience angle.
